Mechanistic Interpretability in Brains and Machines: Shared Concepts and Converging Methods

COSYNE 2026 Workshop March 17, 2026 Cascais, Portugal

About

Interpretability research in neuroscience and AI has independently arrived at a variety of methodological principles for investigating the mechanisms and representations underlying intelligent behavior. While these lines of inquiry have developed in parallel, the pursuit of similar goals has led to the emergence of conceptually analogous methods in both fields, with each emphasizing causality. In this workshop, we will examine the recently developed causal interventions and interpretability methods that facilitate the pursuit of mechanistic understanding of complex cognition.

Our goal is to build a shared understanding by translating and integrating the strongest ideas from each field, emphasizing their complementary perspectives.
