Mechanistic Interpretability in Brains and Machines: Shared Concepts and Converging Methods

COSYNE 2026 Workshop March 17, 2026 Cascais, Portugal

About

This workshop brings together researchers from neuroscience and AI to explore the shared foundations of mechanistic interpretability across biological and artificial neural networks. Despite growing interest in understanding how neural systems compute, a gap remains between the methods and frameworks developed in machine learning interpretability research and those used in systems neuroscience.

Our goal is to unite theorists and experimentalists investigating neural mechanisms, representational geometry, and computational principles — identifying shared concepts and converging methods that can advance both fields.
